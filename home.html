Ken Griffey Jr.

The Man Who Helped Save Baseball in Seattle

Ken Griffey Jr and the Seattle Mariners in 1995 celebrating after his winning run clinched the ALDS against the New York Yankees.


Career Highlights


  • 1987 - Drafted First Overall by the Seattle Mariners
  • 1989 - Made Major League Baseball Debut
  • 1990 - American League All-Star (The first of 13 appeareances)
  • 1990 - American League Gold Glove Winner (The first of 10 Gold Glove Awards)
  • 1992 - Major League All-Star Game Most Valuable Player
  • 1994 - Won first Home Run Derby (The first of three victories)
  • 1997 - American League Most Valuable Player Award (Hit a career high 56 home runs)
  • 2000 - Became the youngest player to ever hit 400 home runs.
  • 2004 - Reached the 500 home run milestone.
  • 2008 - Reached the 600 home run milestone. (Only one of five players to ever reach that mark)
  • 2009 - Retires from Major League Baseball accumulating 630 home runs ranking 6th all-time
  • 2013 - Elected into the Baseball Hall of Fame
